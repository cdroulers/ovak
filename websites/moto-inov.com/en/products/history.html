OVAK SEAT HISTORY

History | Parts | Installation | Applications | Warranty
Phase I - (May 1997)

Fiberglass seat strangely resembling a garden chair with short legs designed for children. (Sorry about that Marie-Michèle). Improvised retention/ejection system.

Trial duration : 350 kilometers (laborious)




Phase II - (June 1997)

Fiberglass seat with footrest and head support using the former retention/ejection system.

Trial duration (900 kilometers) (one foot in the right direction).




Phase III -(August 1997)

Fiberglass seat made from a mold (larger and more esthetic). Same retention/ejection system as prototypes 1 and 2).

Trial duration: 3800 kilometers (not bad at all!).




Phase IV (May 1998)

Over the winter, inventor Michel Martin and designer Gilles Laflamme improved the retention/ejection system. Addition of a removable back sack.

Trial duration: 2700 km.




Phase V - (June 1999)

Introduction of the concept of a seat within a seat.

Integration of the passenger seat with the ejectable shell in three steps. Exterior and interior shells made of fiberglass with ergonomic seat cushion.

Retention/ejection system conceived, produced and tested in engineering trials. In case of serious impact (more or less than 1.5G to the passenger) the interior shell will detach from the exterior shell and provides additional protection for the passenger.

Trial duration: 7000 kilometers to date (wonderful but too heavy and too expensive).




Phase VI- Production

Light and affordable Ovak seat (May 2000)
Exterior shell made of twinsheet thermo-formed polyethelene construction weighing 4 kilos vs. 9 kilos in fiberglass.

Creating of a lighter harness, a dust cover for the seat and an attachable four seasons polar fleece wind breaker.

Trial duration: 10000 kilometers with the collaboration of 4 motorcyclists up to August 1st, 2000.
